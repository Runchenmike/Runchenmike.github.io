Exploration of Moiré Patterns in Two-Dimensional Superlattices

Runchen Li - Stony Brook University, Department of Physics and Astronomy

Introduction

This project investigates quantum phenomena from moiré patterns in 2D superlattices created by stacking graphene and hBN. It aims to manipulate these patterns' properties to explore potential quantum computing applications.

Methodology

  1. Materials are selected and characterized using AFM and optical microscopy.
  2. Stacks are transferred to a flexible substrate, allowing mechanical stress application.
  3. Electrodes measure electronic parameters in a low-temperature, high-magnetic-field environment.

Results and Conclusions

Mechanical stress successfully altered the moiré patterns, demonstrating the feasibility of this approach. Observations include the splitting of the Landau level and secondary Dirac points under strong magnetic fields.

Significance

The research offers a novel method for manipulating 2D materials, contributing to the study of quantum effects and advanced material technology development.

Future Directions

The project will focus on synthesizing and applying 2D materials in advanced electronics and sensors, with an emphasis on interdisciplinary collaborations.
